Platform Overview
🚀What is Units360?

Units360.ai is a low-code usage metering platform. Define any measurable unit — tokens, credits, kWh, API calls, hours — and the platform auto-provisions the ledger, billing APIs, and invoicing logic instantly.

In this demo, Credits are the billing currency. They convert to hot_desk_hour, room_hour, and print_page. When WorkNest changes a conversion rate, all future invoices pick it up automatically.

Use Case
🏢WorkNest — Co-working Space

WorkNest sells monthly credit packages to businesses. Acme Design buys 100 credits/month. Those credits convert to services at defined rates: 1 credit = 1 desk hour, 1 credit = 30 min of a conference room, 1 credit = 10 print pages.

As demand grows, WorkNest can change conversion rates (e.g. conference rooms become more expensive) without rebuilding any billing infrastructure.

Core Concept
🪙Credit — The Billing Currency

A credit is an abstract billing unit. Customers pre-purchase credits; services debit them. This decouples the customer's billing experience from the underlying service pricing.

WorkNest can change service rates (credits required per service unit) without re-negotiating customer contracts. The credit price stays the same; the conversion rate changes.

Service Units
🔄Service Units — What Credits Buy

Each service at WorkNest is also a Units360 unit. When a customer uses a desk or books a room, the booking system fires a CloudEvent. Units360 converts the service usage to credits and debits the account.

hot_desk_hour
1 credit per hour at any desk
room_hour
2 credits per hour (May rate: 1cr=30min)
print_page
0.1 credits per page (10 pages = 1 credit)
Rate versioning: when WorkNest changes room_hour to 4 credits/hour in June, all June invoices apply the new rate. May invoices are already locked at the old rate. No manual recalculation.
Core Concept
🏦Account

An Account is a billing entity — a customer, team, or cost centre. Each account has a balance per unit. For WorkNest, Acme Design's account tracks their credit balance and sub-balances per service.

Core Concept
⚡Transaction

Every service usage event creates an immutable Transaction in the ledger. Each transaction records the unit, quantity, credits debited, and the conversion rate at the time of the event — ensuring accurate historical invoicing.

{
"unit": "hot_desk_hour",

"credits_debited": 1,

"account": "acme-design"
}
Platform Feature
🔄Conversion Rules

Conversion Rules define how service units translate to credits. They are versioned by effective date — a rate change creates a new rule version without invalidating historical records.

"conversions": [
{ "service": "room_hour", "credits": 2,
"effective": "2024-05-01" },
{ "service": "room_hour", "credits": 4,
"effective": "2024-06-01" } // new
]

Units360 applies the correct version for each billing period automatically. No retroactive changes. No manual overrides needed.

Platform Component
📋MDT — Metering Definition Template

The MDT defines your entire metering model: units, accounts, conversion rules, alert thresholds, and billing periods. Submit once; the RTE handles everything else.

Platform Component
⚙️RTE — Real-Time Engine

The RTE processes every CloudEvent in real time: validates the event, applies the correct conversion rule version, debits the account, and updates all ledger balances atomically — all under 50ms.

Rate changes take effect immediately. When WorkNest updates the conference room rate, the RTE picks up the new rule for all events timestamped on or after the effective date. Historical transactions are never modified.
